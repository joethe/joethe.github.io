Playing with virtualization

Being a novice when it comes to servers and linux, I found that I was spending an abysmal amount of time reinstalling things on physical machines after breaking various things in my feeble attempt to learn. Always looking for a means by which to do less work, I decided to look into virtualizing all of my projects.

I began by throwing together some spare parts and an AMD FX6300 (6 cores!) which included a free motherboard as a part of a combo. The result is an abomination with the following specifications.

  • CPU: AMD FX6300 6 core 3.5GHz
  • Motherboard: (will update when I find the box, but it's nothing special)
  • Memory: 4GB of generic DDR3 (single stick)
  • Storage: 500GB generic hard drive
  • Case: (will update when I remember...)
  • Operating System: Proxmox VE

As I'm sure you can see the specifications for this server are far from impressive; it's physical appearance being even less so. However, it still serves adequately as a means by which to experiment with virtualization.

Why Proxmox, Why Not VMware?

I choose Proxmox primarily because of this Tek Syndicate video. Another reason for my choosing Proxmox is that I felt starting out with VMware would be too easy, considering that I wasn't planning on doing anything advanced or complicated for quite some time. That said, now that I've played with Proxmox for awhile I think I can better appreciate VMware; if only for its widespread compatibility.
